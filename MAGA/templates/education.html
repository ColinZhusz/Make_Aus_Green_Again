What makes something recyclable?

Recycling is the process of converting waste into other useable materials. So why are we allowed to put empty milk containers in the recycling bin but not styrofoam packaging? The process of recycling has to be provided with homogenous waste and an economically viable way to collect, process, recycle and re-distribute these materials back into the population.
Recyclable waste is collected from kerbside and then delivered to the appropriate recycling centre. The waste is then processed, sorting the cans, cartons, plastic, glass, etc according to the facilities available at the recycling plant.
Once sorted, the materials are then grouped together and sold onwards to the market. Recycling generates revenue as the raw materials included in the recycling can be bought cheaply and used to manufacture products that have a higher sell price, maximising the profit.

Saving and Making Money

Proper recycling has several monetary benefits as well as improving the quality of life and the environment that we all live in. The monetary Benefits include:

  • Rates and taxes go down if the government get additional funds through proper recycle
  • Several jobs are created with a sustainable recycling process
  • Journal of Waste Management estimates a revenue of over $60 million with proper waste management and recycling schemes
  • Several private companies will pay for your recycling if properly segregated

The Gift of a Green Future

Perhaps the biggest advantage of proper waste disposal and keeping recycling as the focus is the long term effect it has on the environment. With Global Warming affecting the climate, the Earth seems to be heading towards inevitable disaster but proper waste management could help reduce a massive slice of emissions, leaving the Earth safe and fresh for our future generations. Some of the main environmental benefits of proper recycling are given below:

  • Conservation of Energy, and hence, recycling is a renewable source of energy
  • Greenhouse Gases such as Methane, Carbon Dioxide and Carbon Monoxide are emitted minimally
  • Conserves natural resources such as timber, water and minerals
  • Prevents pollution by reducing the need to collect new raw materials

Reduced Carbon Footprint

  • Recycling items saves the energy to create new items
  • The emissions from landfill incinerators is avoided
  • Recycled paper means more trees are left standing
  • Composting helps reduce methane emissions
